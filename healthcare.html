Why I made this app?

I was nominated for a design challenge to test the candidates' UX/UI design skills and work under time pressure and limited time to present a solution. So I accepted the challenge with full willingness, but decided that I would develop the project to its full potential :)

About Project

  • Technology systems are used every day in hospitals and doctors' offices. There is no uniform database of patients to ensure the accuracy of their data and visits.
  • Doctors and nurses manually manage their logs, take written notes and have very little insight into the medical practice's workload.
  • As a result, staff are often frustrated and medical practices suffer significant financial losses due to the inefficiencies of current processes.

My Task:

Creating a digital tool that allows doctors and nurses to manage patient visits and improve communication between the patient and the medical practice.

Source: My physical whiteboard :)

Problem Statement

Lack of a communication system to improve communication and work for medical staff:

Challenges

Create a simple medical system, so that you can quickly facilitate the work of medical practice.

Providing all the functions: patient information, managing appointments, and communicating between patients and staff.

Synchronization of data between health facilities.

Flow diagram

To outline all the necessary functionality, I created a simple flow diagram of the main tasks the user can do, based on the previously created user story.

Wireframes

After creating the flow diagram, I started sketching out low-fidelity wireframes of the main user flow on paper, which I then transferred to a digital version. In the end, I got 75+ medium-fidelity screens that represent the first usability of the application.

Style Guide

Once the initial flow was complete and the wireframes were ready, I started by creating a full style guide and defining styles to speed things up.

Colors

Choosing colors is always a difficult choice. I went for the safest option - blue. Simple blue is one of the colors that inspires a lot of confidence and is associated with responsibility and safety.

Typography

The typography was designed according to the HUI (Human Interface Guidelines). I designed 2 sets of styles - Bold and Regular - to enrich the font hierarchy. I used General Sans as a sans serif typeface to keep the interface modern and clear.

Iconography

Ionicons Icon Pack is a set of open-source icons that fully covers my need for them in this project. It has multi-variant icons, so I was able to choose between - Filled and Outline.

Components

Before designing high-fidelity mockups, I started designing components that would be fully reusable. Many of these components are repetitive, so I created components with many variations of them, which drastically sped up my work. The screens thus maintain a consistent whole throughout the project.I included the components shown in the Design system created from scratch for the Healthcare application. I divided the DS into Atoms, Molecules and Organisms to create an effect where each component builds the next. Below is a snippet of the Healthcare DS.

Buttons

I designed the buttons under 4 states for all the needs of the project. I got 3 main types - Primary, Secondary, Teritary, which represent a hierarchy of elements. The states, on the other hand, are responsible for showing the possibility of an element and adapting the function to the situation.

Inputs

I designed most input types for the login and user registration screens. I took into account situations in which the user makes a mistake, as well as those in which the user proceeds to the next step without encountering any problems.

Cards

I designed the cards starting with the smallest molecule - the atom, going further to molecules, which are reproducible on almost every screen, eventually getting an organism, such as a visit card.

Illustrations

I used a library of illustrations from IconScout prepared by Manypixels Gallery and roundsquid. All icons and illustrations were edited for the project. I redesigned the colors and the elements on them according to the accepted Style Guide.

High-Fidelity UI Design

Based on previous research, designing first sketches and preparing styles and components, I proceeded to design high-fidelity mockups. In the end, I received 85+ screens. Below I have presented a small snippet of two flows - Sign up Flow, Doctor App Flow.

Alignment and Grid

In the design, I picked an 8-point grid. Soft Gird has margins set to 16px and gutter 8px. For the margins inside the groups I used 8 and 16, and the margins between the groups at 24, 32 and 40. In the project, as always, I applied Auto Layout to all elements, which definitely speeds up the work and with the right settings, such as Fill cointainer, makes the project responsive.

High-Fidelity Prototype

I combined my hi-fi screens into a clickable prototype using Figma. Prototyping apps gives us the opportunity to realistically test usability, and by doing so, we are able to save hundreds, if not thousands of $ already at the design stage.

Takeaways

A brief description of what I was able to create.

The resulting application works on mobile devices. According to Statista, 83.32% of the population uses smartphones, making the Healthcare mobile app accessible to most people.

It solves the problem of work organization, which is greatly improved by reading patient information and managing patient appointments.

The patient's mental peace of mind is ensured by being able to quickly contact the doctor and send a message or call.

I decided that I would use the potential of the project and develop the project to the end. All aspects that led to the finished interface is a great experience that builds my skills.
