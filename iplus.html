Why I made the redesign?

Having been a customer of the Plus mobile network for 10 years and using their mobile application since its inception, I decided to introduce some innovations and user-friendliness. The performance of the app is very badly evaluated on both platforms - IOS&Android.

Market Research

The claim

The popularity of mobile networks is growing every year, due to the increasing quality to its price. The growth can be seen, for example, from the increase in mobile Internet users. The network is already available to 99% of the Polish population.

The problem

Old providers have to compete on price, which is particularly noticeable with the Plus network. Very low price to the amount of services offered, but some digital products are of low quality.

Source: UKE

Source: UKE, I quarter 2022

Potential problem

Despite the satisfactory price to the number of services offered, the products are not all of good quality, and one of them is - the iPlus application.

User problems

Old Design trends

Application does not remember login information

90% of users use automatic login, which requires showing the button as a first-class option. Most get lost in the interface unable to find this function.

The user has to go too long to get to the main panel

Entering data such as email, password is made invisible by using invisible lines. The preferred solution - the use of input text fields

Lack of consistency between brand identity and mobile app, such as different colors and styles

What does this information give me?

The survey confirms my earlier assumptions based on user reviews. They are most bothered by the login process, which is too long and causes annoyance in traversing too many screens. And the unchanged look of the app for a long time, causes boredom, and the lack of updates with new experiences makes the interface unreadable.

Personas

I created two personas to help me explore the needs of the app's users - a young person and a person with a wider range of needs.

After going through all my research data, it was time to get down to the actual design of the application, starting with sketching the first flows and initial low-fidelity wireframes

Low-Fidelity Wireframes

After creating the flow diagram, I began sketching low-fidelity wireframes of the main user flow on paper, which I then transferred to a digital version of it.

High-Fidelity UI Design

Once the initial flow was complete and the wireframes were ready, I began to create some main screens for the application. I created a short Style Guide to show the styles used in the project. To build them, I used a plugin in Figma that generates the Material Design color palette - 50-900. To maintain consistency in the interface and hierarchy of elements, I introduced different font weights.

Why did I break the rules?

"Rules are made to be broken." And seriously, the app is fully consistent with the branding, which was recently updated by Plus. Wanting to preserve the existing brand image, I decided to do so.

Let's take a look at the old version

I took a moment to think about the style in which the application should be created. It lacked many factors, one of which was color consistency with the brand. The second thing was the modern look, which in the old version strongly deviated from current trends. The current iPlus app is not WCAG compliant (e.g. buttons)
