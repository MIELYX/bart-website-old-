Why I made the redesign?

Having been a customer of the Plus mobile network for 10 years and using their mobile application since its inception, I decided to introduce some innovations and user-friendliness. The performance of the app is very badly evaluated on both platforms - IOS&Android.

Market Research

What does this information give me?

The survey confirms my earlier assumptions based on user reviews. They are most bothered by the login process, which is too long and causes annoyance in traversing too many screens. And the unchanged look of the app for a long time, causes boredom, and the lack of updates with new experiences makes the interface unreadable.

Let's take a look at the old version

I took a moment to think about the style in which the application should be created. It lacked many factors, one of which was color consistency with the brand. The second thing was the modern look, which in the old version strongly deviated from current trends.

Lots of mistakes during the design of the first version

I made a lot of mistakes when designing the first versions of the iPlus app screens, but practicing has taught me a lot. Yes, it was really ugly, I know, but everyone started at some point :)

Alignment and Grid

In the design, I picked an 8-point grid. I set the main margin to 24. For the margins inside the groups I used 8 and 16, and the margins between the groups at 24, 32 and 40. In the project I used Auto Layout, which strongly speeds up the work, but checking the distances of the elements by a square with widths of 8x.

Usability Testing

After designing the prototype in Figma, I decided to test the introduced solution, and at the same time the biggest concern of users resulting from the survey - reducing the time of user login to the application.

Study details

I created a plan that is based on running a test of the old version of the application and the new one.

I designed the survey on the Maze platform because it offers many possibilities for usability testing.

5 people took part in the survey (Nielsen Norman Group - Research). Not every one of them had used an earlier version of the app or had tested the previous one.

The goal of the study is to see if it is easier for users to see the - go directly to the app button that allows them to quickly log into the app, and if it takes the user less time to reach the main panel by reducing the number of login screens.

Study details

The user selects the "Go to application" option every time because it is the only and fastest option to get to the app. He knows exactly where to click and doesn't wonder how he has to log in through the highlighted auto login button - 0% misclick rate

I have reduced the average time each time a user logs into the app from 13.5 seconds to 3.8 seconds, by removing unnecessary screens that take time away from the user. More than 5,000 negative reviews on Google Play relate to the login process being too long, and the new version solves this problem.

I have created an option to rate both versions of the application - Improvement in average rating from 4.8/10 to 8.3/10. This pulls - increasing the conversion rate

Study details

Checking the performance of biometric login

I would contact the developers of the application to make the application remember the user's data and we would be able to reduce the number of screens to zero! This is the biggest user problem in the app, which would greatly improve the performance of the app.

Accessibility Check

The application was evaluated for contrast and meets full AAA standards. Accessibility was checked with the Figma - Contrast plugin and Color Contrast Checker from Coolors. Unlike the previous version of the app, this one is fully accessible.

CTA Button

This is the app's main CTA, so it must be fully accessible to everyone. This inaccessible button from the first version may have caused problems that strongly affected users.
